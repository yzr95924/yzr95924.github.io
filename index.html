About me

Zuoru Yang currently is a senior research engineer in Storage Product Line at Huawei. He received his Ph.D. degree from Department of Computer Science and Engineering, The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK) in 2022. At CUHK, he was a member of Applied Distributed System Lab (ADSLab), supervised by Prof. Patrick P. C. Lee. Previously, he got his B.Eng. degree from Department of Computer Science and Technology, Xi'an Jiaotong University (XJTU) in 2018. At XJTU, he worked with Prof. Peng Zhang in Software-Defined Network (SDN) and Network Function Virtualization (NFV) (e.g. design debug/verification tools in SDN and NFV).

His research interest is in storage systems, specifically applying data reduction techniques (e.g., deduplication, delta compression, and local compression), data protection techniques (e.g., encryption and trusted computing), and fault tolerance techniques (e.g., erasure coding) in various storage systems for storage efficiency, security, and fault tolerance.
